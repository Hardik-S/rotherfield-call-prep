3. Performance cockpit

Purpose: show crop-year patterns
"This is the quick read on yield, GI/acre, NI/acre, and the GI-NI spread. I would not interpret this as causal. It is a way to see which years deserve follow-up, especially when high yield does not translate cleanly into net income."

What to notice: GI/acre is usually closer to yield than NI/acre because NI includes cost pressure, input mix, and other expense-side factors.

4. Model readout

Purpose: regression without overclaiming
"The regression is a first-pass association check. The question is not 'did yield cause income?' The useful comparison is whether yield explains more variation in gross income per acre than net income per acre. That difference is analytically interesting because net income absorbs costs and timing issues."

Safe phrasing: "The model is directional and exploratory; it helps decide what to investigate next."

5. Inflation method lab

Purpose: turn CPI into a decision
"Right now the dashboard uses a constant-dollar CPI adjustment. That is not the same thing as a formal NPV model. The next methodological choice is whether to show Canada CPI, Ontario CPI, and an agriculture-specific input index side by side, then optionally add NPV if we agree on a discount-rate assumption."

Ask here: "Do you want this to answer purchasing-power comparability, farm-input cost pressure, or investment-style present value?"

6. Benchmark decision track

Purpose: clarify what market average means
"The commodity benchmark is the most important thing to define before building more. Chicago/CME is useful because it is broadly comparable, but it is not a local elevator cash price. Hensall is locally relevant, but we do not yet know whether historical bids are available or complete enough. The question is what comparison you actually want: broad futures context, local selling context, or both."

Key insight: The gap between implied farm price and benchmark includes basis, timing, quality, moisture, freight, storage, FX, and revenue-composition effects.

7. Quality ledger

Likely questions

Answers you can give without bluffing

Why not just use Excel?

"Excel stays the source of truth. The dashboard is for interpretation and review: spotting patterns, comparing methods, and keeping assumptions visible. I added browser-readable workbook tabs so the site does not force anyone away from the spreadsheet."

Why compare GI/acre and NI/acre separately?

"GI/acre is closer to revenue performance from crop sales. NI/acre includes costs, so it can diverge from yield even in strong production years. Comparing both helps separate production performance from cost or margin pressure."

Is CPI the right inflation adjustment?

"It is a defensible baseline for constant-dollar comparison, but it may not capture farm-input cost pressure. That is why I would treat Canada CPI, Ontario CPI, and a Farm Input Price Index as a comparison set before choosing the final primary view."

Is NPV better than CPI?

"They answer different questions. CPI says what past dollars are worth in a common purchasing-power basis. NPV says what future or past cash flows are worth under a discount-rate assumption. I would not add NPV as the main view until we agree on the discount rate and why it fits the farm question."

Why not use Hensall instead of Chicago?

"Hensall may be better for local context if historical bids exist. Chicago/CME is better for a standardized broad market reference. I think the strongest version may show both: CME for comparability and Hensall/local bids for local pricing reality."

Can the dashboard tell whether the farm sold above market?

"Not conclusively yet. It can estimate an implied gross price from GI/acre divided by yield, but that is not the same as exact sale price. The difference from a benchmark includes timing, basis, quality, moisture, storage, freight, FX, and contract timing."

What is the next concrete work?

"I would validate the source data issues, decide the inflation comparison, then build the benchmark data pipeline only after we agree whether the benchmark is meant to be broad-market, local-market, or both."

Term cheat sheet

Definitions to keep in your head

GI/acre

Gross income per acre. Richie confirmed these values are crop-sales revenue only.

NI/acre

Net income per acre. This is more sensitive to cost assumptions and input-price pressure.

Constant-dollar CPI

Adjusts historical dollars to a common purchasing-power year. Useful, but not crop-specific.

NPV / present value

Discounted-cash-flow framing. Requires an explicit discount rate and a reason for using it.

Farm Input Price Index

A better candidate for input-cost pressure than general CPI, especially around fertilizer and chemicals.

CME/Chicago benchmark

Standardized broad market reference. Not the same as local cash price received by the farm.

Hensall/local bid

Potentially more relevant to actual local selling context, but historical availability is unknown.

Basis

The local price difference versus futures/reference price, shaped by location, quality, freight, timing, storage, and other factors.
